However, Mafumafu said that you should pronounce it via individual kanji. 恋話 (Koibana) can be separated into two kanji: 恋 (ren) and 話 (wa), making the title “Keitai Renwa”. The title is a pun on the words “mobile phone” (Keitai Denwa in Japanese), as the title and these words are pronounced very similarly. (2)	The “Hello?” here is not an everyday “hello”, but rather one used on the phone, such as when you pick your phone up and ask “Hello?”. もしもしas a greeting is almost always used on the phone. (3)	As in the second hand on the clock(4)	Darjeeling tea is considered as the champagne of teas due to its sweet aroma and fruity taste that makes it actually taste a bit like champagne, according to Google.
